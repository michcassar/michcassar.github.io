What is Vector Art?


Vector graphics, which are images made using mathematical formulae, are the foundation of vector artwork. In contrast, colourized pixels are used to make raster art, often known as bitmaps or raster images. When you excessively extend pixel-based art in a raster file, it becomes jagged, but you can enlarge vector art to any size without it looking bad. Because of its independence from resolution, vector art may be utilized for everything from tiny graphics to enormous billboards.





Vector vs Raster


The two most often used file types for visual material are raster and vector files. There are several factors to take into account when picking which one to employ because they depict pictures in quite diverse ways. Resolution, usage, file sizes, compatibility, and conversion are some of the key distinctions between raster and vector.
